Eyebrows

MICROBLADING / 3D

Microblading is the most natural looking permanent makeup technique available. Small, crisp , superficial incisions (hair strokes) are placed into the skin mimicking ones natural hair growth pattern. This technique is best suited for clients who have even hair growth throughout both brows. Microblading is not recommended for oily skin types.

OMBRE SHADING

Ombre shading is a light dusting of pigment throughout the front of the brow graduating in color depth towards the tails of the brow to create a soft, powder effect recreating the effect of filling them in with powder makeup.

POWDER BROW

Powdered brows are a bit more dramatic than ombre. It is used to recreate filling them in with pencil or a more dramatic brow style. Shading is done evenly throughout the entire brow.

COMBO-BROW

A combination brow is a combination of a powder or ombre brow and microblading. This technique is best suited for clients who have less hair towards the tail of the brow.

Microblading 3d process & aftercare

Microblading is a two step process. After 4 weeks your brows will have healed and we aim to work with a 60% retention rate. This is based upon our products, pigments and skill used in the initial treatment. If you were to leave your brows at this stage they would still look ok but you would not see the full benefit of a beautiful pair of brows for the full 12-18months. The initial top up at 4-6 weeks allows me to top up the strokes that need a touch up, bringing the pigment back up to 100%.

A few more things to consider BEFORE the microblading treatment, if you have any questions about any of it please just shout:

  • Do not drink coffee, alcohol or energizing drinks on the day of treatment.
  • Do not take Aspirin, Niacin, Vitamin E or Ibuprofen 24 hours before treatment.
  • Do not take omega3 (fish oil) 1 week before treatment.
  • Do not tan or have intense sun exposure 3 days before treatment.
  • No waxing 2-3 days before treatment.
  • No chemical peels, dermabrasion, laser or any other intense treatments like Botox 2 weeks before or after the treatment.
  • Skin should be healthy, non-irritated and blemish free. I cannot blade over spots or Moles.

A Typical Powder / Ombre / Combi brows treatment…

  1. Before the appointment begins, I will provide a quick consultation to discuss expectations and results of the treatment.
  2. I will then draw on the eyebrow shape so brows are measured perfectly to complement your unique face shape and size.
  3. Next, I will choose the perfect color.
  4. Initial strokes are placed, the first pass, then anesthetic is applied to reduce any discomfort.
  5. Pigment is worked into each stroke to define the brow. The Second Pass.
  6. After, a pigment mask is left to absorb into the skin.
  7. Once that’s finished, I will clean the area thoroughly.
  8. After the service, I will apply a balm and you will need to continue with aftercare which I will provide you with. I cannot guarantee your end result! I honestly wish I could , please read below. Do you have…

Very oily skin? Enlarged pores on your forehead/face or acne prone skin? Skin thats very sensitive or reactive? Eczema, psoriasis, rosacea or melasma? Really thick, dark, coarse eyebrow hair?

If you answered yes to any of these – unfortunately, brows will not last very long on you skin and details may fade faster. Please keep this in mind when deciding to get shading by me. You may need additional appointments and/or more frequent touch ups. A shaded brow may last longer and give better results, but results cannot be guaranteed!

Extension Lashes

How to Prepare for Your Eyelash Extension Appointment

Here are 5 tips on how to prepare for your eyelash extension service:
  1. If you wear waterproof mascara, discontinue use 3 days prior to your appointment. The compounds in these products will remain on the lashes, even after properly cleansing. This will prevent the extension adhesive from properly bonding with your lashes and your extensions will not last long.
  2. Arrive with a freshly clean face and impeccably clean lashes. Residual oils and makeup remaining on the lashes will only prevent the adhesive from properly bonding. This point needs to be stressed – do not arrive still wearing eye makeup. Less cleaning. More time. More lashes. It is absolutely acceptable to wear facial and brow makeup, but NO EYE MAKEUP.
  3. Avoid oil based products on and around the eyes. Apply eye cream sparingly, if at all. If you use an oil based makeup remover, make sure that is removed completely from the eyes as well. Oil is the kryptonite to lash adhesive.
  4. Remove your contacts, if you wear them. I recommend my clients wear glasses the day of service. It’s recommended to bring your own case for lenses. Be prepared to wear your glasses after the service, as application of contact solution will wet the lashes. Being contact free is a safety precaution and ensures you leave with happy, healthy eyes and lashes.
  5. lease make sure to inform your therapist of any known eye conditions before your appointment, in cases of infections etc…. this treatment may not be suitable at this time. Happy Lashing!!
Eyelash Extentions Aftercare instructions
  1. Keep visits to a sauna,solarium,steam room and swimming pool to a minimum and not at all during the first 48 hrs. Be very gentle with your eyelashes.
  2. Do not touch your eyelashes or get them wet for the next 24 hrs to allow time for the adhesive to completely dry. After this time, only brush very gently upwards with the lash brush/comb that your therapist will have provided you with.
  3. Avoid hot steam from the shower for the next 48 hrs (cool quick showers or baths).
  4. Do not rub your eyes excessively or tug at the extensions.
  5. Do not use any oil based eye make up remover, and when removing eye make up use a cotton wool bud near the lash line to prevent damaging the bond.
  6. Book in for a fill right after your initial set. Lash extensions are semi-permanent and shed with your natural eyelashes. Typically, you lose between (approximately) 1 and 6 natural lashes, per eye, everyday. This is perfectly normal. You may experience more or less daily shedding depending on your lash shed cycle and home care of your lashes to maintain your lash extensions, book in 2 -4 weeks after your session to touch-up missing lashes or add in more for fullness. It is much more cost effective to keep up with your refill appointments!
Single Eyelash Extensions

Wake up feeling made up! This revolutionary service enhances the length, thickness, and fullness of your natural lashes. One false lash is precisely attached to one natural lash, creating a sexier, more youthful appearance. Eyelash extensions reduce the time spent on beauty routines, are safe for everyday wear, and replace the application of mascara and use of eyelash curlers.

Eyelash extensions are customized to your desires and are completely safe for your natural lashes when properly applied.

Volume Eyelash Extensions

Volume is specific for clients who want a truly voluminous look to their lashes. Clients who have minimal or frail natural lashes are the perfect candidate for this treatment. Volume applies multiple lash extensions to one natural lash, allowing for a more sultry and voluminous look to the eyes. Where some clients are unable to receive the same look with classic, for harm to the natural lash, this is where Volume steps in.

SINGLE VS VOLUME

single lashes are a 1:1 ratio.

Volume lashes are 3(or more) :1

This means that the number of extensions applied will only meet the number of natural lashes you have. If you have naturally thick lashes, this look is great for you. However, if you have very few natural lashes, volume lashes are the choice for you.
